Reading money through balance sheets

Why banks matter?

Most economic models describe an economy where money moves between household, firms and the state.

Banks appear only as intermediaries - transferring existing money from savers to borrowers.

In reality, banks do something fundametally different. They create money when they issue loans.

If banks are excluded from the model, the mechanism of money creation disappears.

This site uses publicly available balance sheet data
from central bank and financial institutions.

It does not provide financial advice.
It does not forecast markets.

Its purpose is to reconstruct how money is created
within the banking system.

Modern economic models an economy without banks.
That is why financial crises keep repeating.

Money is not transferred between agents.
It is crated by banks - an institutional fact visible in balance sheets,
yet largely absent from economic models.
