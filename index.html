Reading money through balance sheets

Modern economic models an economy without banks.
that is why financial crises keep repeating.

Money is not transferred between agents.
It is created by banks - an institutional fact visible in balance sheets,
yet largely absent from economic models.

This site uses publicly available balance sheet data
from central bank and financial institutions.

It does not provide financial advice.
It does not forecast markets.

Its purpose is to reconstruct how money is created
within the banking system.

Banks are not intermadiaries.

Standart macroeconomic models treat money as a circulating object between agents - household,firms and the state - while excluding banks as balance-sheet creators.

Banks appear only as intermediaries - transferring existing money from savers to borrowers.

In reality, banks do something fundametally different. They create money when they issue loans.

If banks are excluded from the model, the mechanism of money creation disappears.

How a loan creates money?

Bank balance sheet after loan issuance:

Assets            | Liabilities
------------------|----------------
Loan +100         | Deposit +100

A bank does not lend existing deposits. It creates new money when it issues a loan.

Why balance sheets matter?

Economic models usually focus on flows: income, spending, investment. Balance sheets, however, record stocks: assets, liabilities and equity. Money is soehow a stock, not a flow. If balance sheets are ignored, money itself becomes invisible.

Flows vs stocks.

Macroeconomic textbooks describe the economy as a circulation of money. But circulation diagrams hide the institutiona structure that creates money. Balance sheets make this structure explicit.

Two ways to represent the economy

FLOW-BASED VIEW (standard models)

Households  --->  Firms
     ^              |
     |              v
   Wages         Investment

Money circulates between agents.
Banks are omitted or reduced to intermediaries.


BALANCE-SHEET VIEW (institutional reality)

Households        Banks
 Deposits  <---  Loans
                    |
                    v
              Balance sheets

Money appears as a bank liability.
Creation and destruction are accounting events.
