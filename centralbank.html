The central bank does not create money for the public

This is where confusion usually peaks. People assume the central bank "prints money" and hands it to the economy.

That is not what central banks do. Have you ever heard about M0 or M1?

What the central bank actually creates

The central bank creates reserves. Reserves exist only between banks and the central bank. Of course the central bank prints money physically, but not all the time.

Central bank 
Assets | Liabilities
----------------------
Bonds  | Reserves +100
Commercial bank 
Assets        | Liabilities
---------------------------
Reserves +100 | Equity

Reserves are not deposits. Households and firms cannot use reserves to pay each other.

Where money for the public comes from

Money used by households and firms appears when commercial banks issue loans.

The central bank is not involved in this process directly.

Banks do not wait for reserves to lend. They lend first — and obtain reserves later if needed.

Why this distinction matters

If money were created by the central bank, credit cycles would be policy accidents.

But if money is created by commercial banks, instability is endogenous.

Crises are not surprises. They are balance-sheet dynamics.
