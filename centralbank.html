Step 5 of 5 - Central bank

What the central bank actually does

The central bank does not create money the way commercial banks do. It operates on a different layer of the monetary system.

Two layers of money

Modern monetary systems have two distinct layers:

  • Bank deposits — used by households and firms
  • Central bank reserves — used only by banks

These layers are connected, but they are not the same thing.

Reserves are not deposits

CENTRAL BANK
------------------------

• Bank reserves

COMMERCIAL BANK
------------------------
Assets:

Liabilities:

When a bank creates a loan, it creates a deposit. No central bank money is involved at that moment.

Why control is indirect

Central banks influence interest rates, liquidity conditions and regulations. But they do not decide who gets loans or how much money banks create.

Money creation is decentralized. Control is indirect. Instability is structural.

You have now seen the mechanism

Money appears and disappears through balance sheets. Not through preferences. Not through equilibrium conditions.

Once banks are included, financial crises stop being mysterious.
